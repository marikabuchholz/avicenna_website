Submission Guidelines

At present, we are soliciting submissions (original, previously non-published articles from current university students, faculty, and affiliates) for our May 2014 issue. Our goal is to create a platform for offering thought-provoking perspectives and discussion on topics of pressing importance. Below are some examples of issues we are interested in addressing in the Journal:
· Contemporary (e.g., the debate on freedom of religious expression in light of the French ban on the niqaab, face-veil) · Historical (e.g., a timeline of religious pluralism in Al-Andalus or a piece on Avicenna) · Cultural (e.g., the role of Islam in contesting the cultural attitudes towards women in modern-day Saudi Arabia, translation of poetry or prose) · Legal (e.g., the application of Sharia in a non-Muslim society) · Political (e.g. contemporary and past Muslim reformers) · Spiritual (e.g., love for the Creator as depicted in a selection of Sufi poetry)
Submissions may range from analyses of these issues to interviews with scholars, thinkers and leaders, to selections of poetry. Of course, the format and subject matter is not merely limited to the above. Articles should be no more than 4 pages in length, single-spaced, 12 pt. Times New Roman font.
Avicenna – The Stanford Journal on Muslim Affairs will be distributed on campus at Stanford and will also be available in an electronic version. The deadline for first draft submissions for the May 2014 issue is April 13th, 2014.
Please contact avicenna.stanford@gmail.com in order to submit articles or if you have any questions and/or queries for us.
